JSSIS Sciematics 2019

The all popular JSSIS Sciematics-2019 took off on 30th April 2019. Objectives is
->To provide opportunity to students to work collaboratively,
->To stimulate creativity, promote innovation and research among students,
->To challenge students to use their mathematical and scientific aptitude in solving real life problems.

Sciematics includes exploration of skills in various fields like: Debating, DIY’s, Innovation out of waste, Poster making, Quiz, Seminars, Extempore. Students demonstrate research and critical thinking, innovation and collaborative skills.
This year JSSIS Sciematics showed a participation of around 15 schools. Teams of various schools took on a number of challenging mathematical problems, testing the team’s skills in a range of subject areas, including functions, probability, trigonometry, logarithms and basic geometry. The junior students impressed the group of judges with their math concepts, representing mathematical applications in real world scenarios such as road networks, bridges, time and even crime scene investigations. It was really wonderful to see so many students with a strong interest in the STEM subjects backed by significant knowledge. The students were questioned on mathematics, chemistry, physics, biology, anatomy, geology, astronomy, and the history of science, and in nearly every case were able to answer more than 50% of the questions with one team scoring a perfect 100%. As always, the questions set were above and beyond the knowledge of their own curriculum and certainly gave most of the adults in the room a test. But the children took the challenge in their stride and battled it out. It was wonderful to see our sister school JSS Private School bag the overall trophy while JSSIS was the runner up for JSSIS Sciematics 2019.
